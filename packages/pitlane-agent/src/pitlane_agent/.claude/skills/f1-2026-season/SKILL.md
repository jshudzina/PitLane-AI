# 2026 F1 Season Reference

This skill exists to prevent parametric memory errors. The 2026 season has sweeping regulation changes that differ significantly from recent years. Always consult these facts before answering current-season questions.

## Reigning Champion

- **2025 World Drivers' Champion: Lando Norris (McLaren)**
- Norris secured the title in the final race at Abu Dhabi, finishing third to win by 2 points from Max Verstappen
- This was Norris' first world championship and McLaren's first WDC in 17 years
- It ended Verstappen's four-year consecutive championship streak (2021–2024)
- **2025 Constructors' Champion: McLaren**

## Grid Size and Teams

- **22 drivers** on the 2026 grid (up from 20)
- **11 constructors** — Cadillac joined as the 11th team

### New for 2026
| Team | Engine | Drivers |
|------|--------|---------|
| Cadillac | Ferrari | Sergio Pérez, Valtteri Bottas |
| Audi | Audi (rebranded from Kick Sauber) | Nico Hülkenberg, Gabriel Bortoleto |

### Key Driver Moves
- **Isack Hadjar** → Red Bull Racing (replacing Tsunoda; Tsunoda moved to VCARB/Racing Bulls)
- **Franco Colapinto** → Alpine
- McLaren, Mercedes, Ferrari, Williams, Aston Martin, Haas maintained 2025 lineups

## Qualifying Format

The 22-car grid changes the elimination structure compared to the 20-car format:

| Session | Duration | Cars in | Cars eliminated | Cars advancing |
|---------|----------|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Q1 | 18 min | 22 | 6 (positions 17–22) | 16 |
| Q2 | 15 min | 16 | 6 (positions 11–16) | 10 |
| Q3 | 12 min | 10 | 0 (no elimination) | — |

**Key difference from 2025:** In 2025 (20 drivers), Q1 eliminated 5 → 15 advanced to Q2. In 2026, Q1 eliminates **6** → **16** advance to Q2.

## Power Unit Regulations

### MGU-H Eliminated
- The MGU-H (Motor Generator Unit-Heat) has been **completely removed** from 2026 power units
- Do NOT mention MGU-H failures, deployment, or issues for 2026 or later seasons
- There is no heat energy recovery system in 2026

### New Electrical Architecture
- **MGU-K output: 350kW** (up from 120kW in 2025)
- Near **50/50 split** between ICE (~400kW) and electrical (350kW) power
- Battery can recharge more than double previous 4MJ per lap
- All power units run **Advanced Sustainable Fuels** (carbon capture, waste, non-food biomass sources)

### Power Unit Cost Cap
- Increased from $95m to **$130m** to support new development costs

## Aerodynamics — Active Aero Replaces DRS

- **DRS (Drag Reduction System) has been eliminated**
- Replaced by **Active Aerodynamics** — front and rear wings automatically adjust on designated straights
  - **X-Mode**: Low-drag mode (straights) — wings flatten for reduced drag
  - **Z-Mode**: High-downforce mode (corners) — wings return to normal
- No driver button activation; the system is **automatic**
- This changes how overtaking works: no DRS zone activation, no DRS train dynamics

## Car Dimensions and Weight

| Dimension | 2025 | 2026 | Change |
|-----------|------|------|--------|
| Minimum weight | 798 kg | **768 kg** | −30 kg |
| Wheelbase | 3,600 mm | **3,400 mm** | −200 mm |
| Width | 2,000 mm | **1,900 mm** | −100 mm |
| Downforce | — | ~30% less | Significant reduction |
| Drag | — | ~55% less | Significant reduction |

- Narrower front and rear tyres (Pirelli redesigned)
- Larger diffuser openings; ground-effect tunnels removed
- Rear wing: 3-element design; lower beam wing removed
- Strengthened roll hoop and two-stage nose cone for safety

## Calendar

- **24 races** total
- **Madrid** (Madring street circuit) added — Spanish GP Round 2, September. 5.4 km, 22 corners
- **Imola** removed (Emilia Romagna GP contract not renewed)
- Spain now hosts two rounds: Barcelona (traditional) and Madrid (new)
- Italy retains one round: Monza

## Points System

- Fastest Lap bonus point: **removed** (eliminated in 2025, still absent in 2026)
- Maximum points for a race win: **25**
- Sprint race maximum: **8**
- No change to standard 25-18-15-12-10-8-6-4-2-1 race points structure
